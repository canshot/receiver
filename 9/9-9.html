Can book piracy be stopped?

When we surveyed readers aged between 20 and 70, most regularly downloaded books illegally and while some felt guilty – more than one said they only pirated “big names” and when “the author isn’t on the breadline, think Lee Child” – the majority saw nothing wrong in the practice.

Most readers don't buy books due to constraints in funding or are not being able to visit libraries due to mental or physical constraints. Especially when books are available online for free to them, readers prefer that over physically buying a book. 
While some readers think that having the physical copy of the book makes it feel a lot better to read, most of them do not have a strong opinion against piracy of books.

This however, is taking a toll on the upcoming authors and publishers costing them “billions of dollars annually”. It is also hard to quantify how bad the problem is, when so few publishers are willing to talk openly about it.

To read more about how piracy has become a huge concern for the author, click here.

Let's start like a small drop of water in the ocean and take that ONE LITTLE STEP and help authors earn for what they've put effort for, fair and square. It's only fair, as we readers are whom they rely on for their living.
